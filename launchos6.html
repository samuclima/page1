The questions you actually have

Yes, we've heard about the last guy.

The objections below are the ones we hear from every single founder on their first call. Straight answers.

What happens if you take my money and ghost me, like the last freelancer did?
There is no upfront deposit. You pay $349 on day 1 — the same as month 2, month 3, and every month after. If we don't ship the full build by day 5, the next month is free, automatically credited to your card. If we still haven't shipped after that, you walk away with everything we've built so far and a full refund. The Live Build Tracker shows you exactly what's happening every single day, so "going dark" isn't a possibility.
I bought HighLevel/HubSpot/Kajabi and never figured it out. Will I have to learn another tool?
No. Your operator does 100% of the configuration. You never map a workflow, write a line of code, configure branching logic, or watch a tutorial. We use a CRM under the hood, but you only see the simple inbox view: who called, when, what they want, click to call back. That's it. If you can read a text message, you can use this.
Are these going to be shared leads like Angi sells?
No. LaunchOS builds the infrastructure that captures your own leads from your own marketing — your Google profile, your ads, your website, your referrals. Every lead is exclusive to your business. Nobody else gets the same call. You own the CRM, the contact list, and the entire flow. If you cancel, you keep all of it.
Do I own everything, or am I locked into your platform?
You own everything. The domain, the landing page, the contact list, the CRM data, the email sender reputation — all of it is in accounts you own with login credentials you control. We're the operator, not the landlord. Cancel and you keep working the infrastructure yourself, hire another operator, or hand it to a friend's nephew. Your call.
Why is this so much cheaper than every agency I've talked to?
Because we don't run your ads, write your blog, manage your social, or do account management calls. We build infrastructure — a finite, productized scope — and then maintain it. Agencies charge $4–10k/mo because they're billing labor hours on never-ending projects. LaunchOS is a finished thing you own, with a maintenance fee. That's why it's $349 instead of $4,000.
Who actually builds this? Is it AI?
A real human operator with a name, a face, and a Slack handle. You'll know who's on your build by the end of day 1. We use software to move fast, but the decisions, the copy, the configurations, and the QA are all done by a person who is accountable to your account specifically. No anonymous "team" emails. No offshore relay race.
